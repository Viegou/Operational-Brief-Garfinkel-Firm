SECTION 1 — PRODUCT BASICS (Memorize This)

IUL — Index Universal Life (Premium Product)

✓ Market-tied (7-8% growth potential)
✓ Living Benefits (pays out if you get heart attack/stroke/cancer while alive)
✓ Best for: Healthy clients who want growth + protection
✓ Example: ~$50/month for $10k coverage on 70-year-old

Whole Life (For Sick Clients)

📌 Fixed 3% growth (no market upside)
📌 More expensive than IUL
📌 Death only (no living benefits)
📌 Best for: Sick/unhealthy people who can't qualify for IUL
📌 Example: ~$100/month for $10k coverage on 70-year-old

PMI vs. Mortgage Protection — KEY DIFFERENCE

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance): Protects the BANK if you default

Mortgage Protection: Protects YOUR FAMILY. Pays off the house if you die or get critically ill.

Most people don't know this difference. Use it as a sales hammer.

SECTION 3 — KEY CONCEPTS & PIVOTS

The "Incomplete File / Junior Agent" Pivot

When they say: "I already have a policy with [Carrier]."

Response: "That's exactly why I'm calling. You likely spoke to a junior agent, and your file is sitting on my desk as incomplete. Keith is the Senior Underwriter. He just needs 5 minutes to verify they didn't overcharge you based on the new 2026 rates."

The "Ghost" Analogy (Deepening the Why)

Script: "Let's say you passed away yesterday, and I'm talking to your ghost right now. We are looking at this from the outside in. What is your wife doing for money to pay the mortgage today?"

Use when they're soft on urgency. This hits real fear.

The "Think About It" Metric (Price Problem)

If Keith builds the emotional "Why" and the lead still says "I need to think about it," it means the quoted price was too high.

SECTION 4 — HEALTH UNDERWRITING QUICK GUIDE

Red Flags That Force Whole Life

  • Heart disease / heart attack history
  • Stroke history
  • Cancer (any type)
  • Diabetes (Type 1 or poorly controlled Type 2)
  • Kidney/liver disease
  • Heavy tobacco use
  • Poor credit / financial instability

Green Lights For IUL

  • No major health issues
  • Non-smoker
  • Good credit / stable finances
  • Under 75 years old (generally)
