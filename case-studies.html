CASE STUDIES

Keith Garfinkel — Real Leads, Real Pivots, Real Closes

Real Lead Profiles
Case #1 — The Miner (Debt Consolidation Play)
Disabled, 6 whole-life policies, wife has Parkinson's
Problem: Six whole-life policies = $500+/mo drain. Junior agents sold terrible coverage.
Objection: Already has coverage. Too many payments.
Keith's Pivot: Pitched as a Financial Advisor (leveraging his finance degree). Strategy: Consolidate existing policies, eliminate high-interest debt, and optimize for Living Benefits specifically for wife's health crisis.
The Close: Miner didn't buy another policy — he bought a debt consolidation + protection strategy. Felt heard.
📌 Lesson: Sometimes you're not selling insurance. You're selling financial strategy.
Case #2 — Josie Todd (Grandson Angle)
Talked in circles, complained about $80/mo, wanted coverage for 23-year-old grandson
Problem: $165k owed on home, only $170k coverage. Grandson's whole-life policy was a ripoff ($40/mo for $10k).
Objection: Too expensive. Not interested.
Keith's Pivot: Ignored Josie's noise about her own payment. Focused on the grandson. Pitched an IUL for grandson at fraction of his current whole-life premium.
The Close: By solving for the grandson, Josie felt like a hero protecting family. Her own payment objection disappeared.
📌 Lesson: Find the emotional trigger. Sometimes it's not about them — it's about who they love.
Case #3 — Vanessa Lewis (The Angry Lead)
Rude, impatient, "I don't got time for this shit"
Problem: Angry leads hang up on soft salespeople immediately.
Instinct: Zach would have backed down. Keith stayed aggressively direct.
Keith's Response: Zero apologies. No corporate speak. Just: "Vanessa, I get it — you're busy. I don't need 30 minutes. I need 5. If you have a mortgage, your wife deserves to know what happens if you die today. So let's keep it short."
The Close: She booked. Angry leads don't hate salespeople — they hate fake politeness. Keith's directness built trust.
📌 Lesson: Never apologize unnecessarily. Match their energy. Straight-shooters respect straight-shooters.
Case #4 — Curtis Pleasant (The Paranoia Play)
Terrified of "Suspected Spam" tags, doesn't trust calls
Problem: "How do I know you're real?"
Keith's Pivot: Offered to text Keith's state license number and official ID photo to verify identity as firm owner.
The Close: Paranoia → Proof → Booking. Curtis felt validated.
📌 Lesson: Use documentation as trust-building. Sometimes you need to prove you're not a scammer.
Case #5 — Rene Lopez (The Incomplete File)
Had Forrester's for a year, said he was all set
Problem: "I already have coverage."
Zach's Pivot: Used the "Junior Agent / Incomplete File" attack: "That's exactly why I'm calling. You likely spoke to a junior agent, and your file is sitting on my desk as incomplete. Keith is the Senior Underwriter. He just needs 5 minutes to verify they didn't overcharge you based on new 2026 rates."
The Close: Positioned as a REVIEW, not a replacement sale. Rene booked because he wanted to ensure he wasn't overpaying.
📌 Lesson: "Incomplete file" is gold. Use it when they already have coverage.
Case #6 — Tracy McCulley (Multi-Decision Maker)
Constantly busy, rescheduled multiple times, needs mother on call
Problem: Can't make decisions without mom. Keeps rescheduling.
The Lesson: Do NOT accept "I'll check with my mom and call you back." Get mom on the phone NOW or appointment dies.
Resolution: Zach forced a 3-way call immediately. Both locked in. No more reschedules.
📌 Lesson: Multi-decision makers must be locked down upfront. No callbacks. Ever.
Case #7 — Donnie (The Truck Driver)
Over-the-road driver, wife handles mail, needs morning calls
Problem: Not home during day. Wife is primary. Schedule is non-standard.
The Lesson: Profile your lead by occupation. Truck drivers, nurses on night shift, etc. have non-standard availability. Ask first.
Resolution: Booked for 6:30am (before his route). Locked him in with security code. No-show prevention.
📌 Lesson: Availability matters. Know who you're calling and when they're actually home.
Case #8 — Camilla Abdulbar (The Liar/Guaranteed Issue)
Lied about health history, denied by all major carriers
Problem: Denied by Mutual of Omaha, Foresters, AND Transamerica.
Keith's Pivot: Shifted her to a Guaranteed Issue policy (no health exam, no underwriting). Higher premium, but she qualified.
The Close: Camilla got covered. Keith took the commission.
📌 Lesson: If major carriers deny → pivot to Guaranteed Issue (American Amicable as LAST resort).
